## SECTION 8 — WHAT'S AT STAKE
> Background: white | 2×3 grid of tiles | Teal icon or number per tile

**SECTION HEADLINE:**
What's at Stake

**TILE 1 — FAIR HOUSING RIGHTS**
Without enforcement, the Fair Housing Act is just words on paper. Real families — denied loans, turned away from housing, trapped in unsafe conditions — have nowhere left to turn.

**TILE 2 — LANGUAGE ACCESS**
Millions of Americans who do not speak English as their first language depend on language access protections to assert their legal rights. Those protections are being stripped without explanation.

**TILE 3 — WORKER INTEGRITY & FREE SPEECH**
Federal workers are being punished for following the law. When public servants cannot speak up about illegal conduct, corruption hides in silence — and the public pays the price.

**TILE 4 — DEMOCRATIC ACCOUNTABILITY**
Government agencies that ignore the law don't fail by accident. When enforcement is politicized, agencies stop serving the people and start serving whoever holds power.

**TILE 5 — PROTECTION FROM DISCRIMINATION**
Fair housing law protects people on the basis of race, gender, religion, age, disability, and national origin. Dismantling enforcement is not a policy choice — it is a civil rights violation.

**TILE 6 — GOVERNMENT FOR THE PEOPLE**
When agencies are gutted and their missions abandoned, power concentrates among the wealthy and well-connected. Without independent enforcement, government becomes a tool of the powerful — not a protection for everyone else.
